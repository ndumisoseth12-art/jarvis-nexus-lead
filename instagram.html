The Instagram playbook.

How to run NSM’s Instagram so it earns trust before a prospect ever replies. This is a credibility surface, not a vanity project — every post should make a sceptical SA business owner think “this guy is real and he gets my problem.” Post 3–4×/week, stay honest, and let the cold outreach do the closing.

// Section 01

The play

IG isn’t where you close — it’s where you become believable. When you DM or email a prospect and they tap your profile, the page has to confirm: real person, real work, understands SA small business.

  • Goal: a profile that makes a cold prospect trust you in 10 seconds — and a slow trickle of warm inbound DMs.
  • Not the goal: follower count, viral reels, or posting for the algorithm. Ignore vanity metrics.
  • The test for every post: would this make a salon owner / plumber / dentist think “he actually gets it”? If not, don’t post it.
  • Your edge: you’re 19, building solo in Joburg, in public. Lean into it — that’s the opposite of a faceless agency, and it’s why people will root for you.
// Section 02

Content pillars

Five buckets. Rotate them so the feed mixes proof, teaching, and personality. Roughly: 40% build-in-public, 25% pain/teaching, 20% bot demos, 15% founder.

1 · Build in public

~40% — what you shipped this week, screenshots of a real workflow, a bug you fixed, a milestone. Proof you’re actively building, not coasting. Ties to the homepage build-log.

2 · SA SMB pain

~25% — relatable problems your buyer feels: “R6,000/mo walking out to no-shows”, the 2am review, the invoice nobody chased. Name the pain better than they can.

3 · Bot demos

~20% — 15–30s screen recordings of one bot doing one job (a no-show rebook firing, a review reply drafting). Show, don’t tell. Sample data only.

4 · Founder

~15% — you. Studying electrical engineering, building this between classes, why you started, the aunt with the salon. Human, honest, no flexing.

5 · Results (later)

ONLY once real pilots land — a genuine before/after with the client’s permission. Until then, zero results posts. No fake numbers, ever.

// Section 03

Weekly cadence

3–4 feed posts + light stories. Consistency beats volume — better 3 solid posts than 7 rushed ones.

  • Mon — Build-in-public: “Shipped this week” (mirror the homepage build-log).
  • Wed — Pain/teaching carousel: one SA SMB problem + how it gets solved.
  • Fri — Bot demo reel (15–30s) OR founder post, alternating.
  • Stories — 2–3×/week: behind-the-scenes, a poll (“how do you handle no-shows?”), repost a reply. Keep it light.
  • Best times (SA): weekday mornings 7–9am or evenings 7–9pm. Post, then reply to every comment for the first hour.

// Section 08

Rules — don’t break these

  • No fake results, fake clients, or “trusted by” until real pilots land (same rule as the site).
  • No real customer data in demos — sample data only, and say so. POPIA + trust.
  • No cold-DM spam to strangers. DM warm replies and people who engage — not random businesses (IG limits + it reads desperate).
  • Don’t buy followers or engagement. A small real audience > a big fake one for a credibility play.
  • No AI-buzzword bingo (“revolutionary”, “10x”). Plain language, real pain.
  • Reply to every genuine comment & DM within a day. The whole point is being reachable and real.
North star: post like the honest 19-year-old builder you are. The feed’s only job is to make “is this guy legit?” answer itself before they even reply.
