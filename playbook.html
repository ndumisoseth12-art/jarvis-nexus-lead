B1 · Lead Catcher Phase 3 · Coming Soon

WhatsApp Lead Catcher.

// THE PROBLEM
After-hours WhatsApp enquiries pile up overnight. By the time you reply at 8am, the prospect has already gone to a competitor. SA SMBs lose 40-60% of inbound WhatsApp leads to slow replies — and most owners don't even know how many they're losing.
// HOW IT WORKS
01
Customer messages your WhatsApp number

Any enquiry, any hour. "Hi, do you do braids on Saturdays?" or "What's your price for a 2-bedroom rental?"

02
The bot reads the message in under 10 seconds

AI parses what they want, picks up urgency signals ("now", "today", "asap"), and detects the service they're asking about.

03
Lead is scored — Hot, Warm, or Cold

Hot = high-intent + ready to buy. Warm = qualified but needs nurturing. Cold = info-only.

04
Personalised reply sent in your tone

For Hot leads, the bot sends pricing + a booking link. For Warm, qualifying questions. For Cold, a friendly info reply.

05
You get a Telegram alert + calendar event

Owner is pinged on every Hot lead within 60 seconds. Calendar entries auto-created when a meeting is booked.

// SPECS
Who triggers it
Your customer (sends WhatsApp message)
What you see
Telegram alert · WhatsApp auto-reply log · Calendar event
Best for
Estate agents, salons, plumbers, freelancers — any service where leads come in via WhatsApp after hours

B2 · Churn Radar Live

Churn Radar.

// THE PROBLEM
Most SMBs only notice a customer is churning 90 days after they're already gone — by which point win-back is hard. You need to spot the slip before it happens, while they still feel like a customer.
// HOW IT WORKS
01
Every day at 06:00 SAST, the bot scans your customer activity

Pulls the latest data from your customer Google Sheet — last purchase, frequency, average spend.

02
Each customer is scored on three signals

Recency (how long since last action), Frequency (how often they normally come back), Spend Drop (drop vs their normal).

03
"At risk" customers are flagged

Anyone above the churn threshold is marked for action — typically 2-5 customers per day for a mid-sized SMB.

04
AI drafts a personal "we miss you" message for each

References what they bought last, the time gap, and offers a context-appropriate reason to come back.

05
You get the list in your morning Owner Brief

One Gmail at 07:00. Tap a name, send the message. Three minutes of your day, churn rate down 20-30%.

// SPECS
Who triggers it
Daily cron — no customer action needed
What you see
Morning Gmail brief with at-risk list + suggested messages
Best for
Subscription businesses, gyms, salons, restaurants — anywhere with repeat customers
Available on
StarterOperatorCommand
B3 · No-Show Rescue Live

No-Show Rescue.

// THE PROBLEM
South African salons, dentists, and clinics lose 20-40% of revenue to no-shows. A missed slot is dead time you can't reclaim — and the customer often won't reschedule on their own. Most won't even apologise.
// HOW IT WORKS
01
Booking comes in

From your booking system (Fresha, Setmore, custom form) via webhook, or you book manually via the operator form.

02
Bot schedules two reminders automatically

24 hours before the appointment, and 2 hours before — emailed and (in Phase 3) sent on WhatsApp.

03
One-tap confirm or reschedule

Customer can confirm with a click. Or, if life happens, they can tap "reschedule" and pick a new slot without phoning.

04
If they no-show, instant rescue message

The moment a no-show is marked, the customer gets a friendly "we missed you — want to rebook?" with a fresh slot picker.

05
You see recovery rate in your brief

How many bookings, how many no-shows, how many rescued. Owners typically rescue 20-40% of no-shows back to a booked slot.

// SPECS
Who triggers it
Operator (or booking system via webhook /noshow-book)
What you see
Customer email reminders · Telegram on no-show · Recovery rate in brief
Best for
Salons, dentists, clinics, beauty therapists — any business with appointment slots

B6 · Reputation Shield Live

Reputation Shield.

// THE PROBLEM
One bad public Google or Hello Peter review can cost months of marketing spend. By the time you see it, it's already public — and replying defensively only makes it worse. You need to catch unhappy customers before they go public.
// HOW IT WORKS
01
Trigger after every customer interaction

Manual trigger after a service done, or automatic via POS webhook on completed sale.

02
Customer gets a private feedback request first

"How was your experience? Tap a number 1-5." Not "leave us a review on Google" — that comes later, conditionally.

03
Branch on rating

4-5★: redirected to Google / Hello Peter review link (so happy customers leave public reviews). 1-3★: private form asking what went wrong.

04
You get Telegram alert on low scores

The instant a 1-3 lands, you're pinged with the customer name + feedback. You have an hour to call them before they go cold.

05
You fix it — they don't go public

Most customers who feel heard don't post a public bad review. The negative becomes a private fix, not a permanent stain.

// SPECS
Who triggers it
Operator (after service) or POS webhook /reputation-trigger
What you see
Customer feedback form · Telegram on low scores · Private feedback log
Best for
Service businesses, hospitality, restaurants — anywhere reviews drive new business
Available on
