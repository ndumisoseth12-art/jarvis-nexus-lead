// BUSINESS OWNERS ASK ME

The questions every owner has before signing up.

Honest answers. Tap any question.

What kind of business is this built for? +

Any SA business that invoices customers and has repeat-purchase relationships. Best fits: accountants, bookkeepers, attorneys, freelancers, electricians, plumbers, small B2B services, e-commerce stores, subscription businesses. If you've ever said "I should follow up on that invoice" and then forgot for 3 weeks — this is for you.

How does the bot know who counts as "lapsed" vs just quiet? +

During onboarding we set a per-customer baseline based on their historical cadence. A monthly buyer who skips 60 days is lapsed. A quarterly buyer at 60 days is normal. Churn Radar uses each customer's own pattern, not a generic threshold — that's the whole point. You can override any baseline in your sheet whenever you want.

Will the bot harass my customers and damage relationships? +

No. Every message is written in your voice — warm, professional, not aggressive. Revenue Recovery has hard caps (max 3 chases over 21 days, then it stops and pings you to escalate manually). Reactivation only sends one winback per 90-day window. You can pause any customer's flow in one click if a personal situation comes up. The bots are polite; you stay in control.

What if my invoices live in QuickBooks / Xero / Sage? +

QuickBooks and Xero have native integrations we can set up during onboarding (it pulls open invoices automatically). For Sage, we use a Google Sheets bridge that syncs daily — same end result, slightly slower setup. If you invoice from Word/Excel manually, we'll set you up with a simple sheet that the bots watch. Honest about it: bring your current system to the demo and I'll tell you what's automatic vs manual today.

Can it handle international clients or multiple currencies? +

Yes for ZAR, USD, EUR, GBP out of the box. The bot reads each invoice's currency and adjusts message phrasing accordingly. For unusual currencies (NGN, KES, etc.) we can configure them — flag this on the demo call. If you have non-English-speaking clients, the LLM can also draft chase emails in their language as a paid add-on.

What if a customer disputes the invoice mid-chase? +

The bot detects "this is wrong" / "I don't owe this" / "please call me" replies and immediately stops the chase, pings you on Telegram, and flags the invoice for personal handling. The bot will never argue with a customer or escalate a dispute. That's a human conversation, every time.

How is this different from a debt collector? +

Two things. First, tone: debt collectors are confrontational; this is your own polite voice. Second, cost: debt collectors take 25–40% of what they recover; the Recovery Pack is a flat R1,990/month regardless of volume. If a single invoice goes to true legal collection, the bot stops chasing and hands off to you — that's a relationship to handle with a lawyer, not a bot.

What about my long-term enterprise clients? +

You can mark VIP clients in your sheet. For those, the bot won't auto-chase — instead it pings you on Telegram ("MTN Group invoice is 7 days overdue — want me to draft a chase, or will you call?"). For your top relationships, the bot becomes an assistant, not a collector. You decide who gets the human touch.

Can the bot chase via WhatsApp instead of email? +

Right now the chase ladder runs through email only. Our WhatsApp Business integration (B1 Lead Catcher) launches Phase 3 — we're waiting on the SA WhatsApp Business account approval from Meta. Until then, email is the right format for overdue invoices anyway — your client can forward the email (with EFT details + PayFast link) to their bookkeeper in one tap, which is harder to do from WhatsApp. When WhatsApp goes live, your account gets upgraded at no extra cost.
