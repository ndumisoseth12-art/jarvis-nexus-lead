// PRACTITIONERS ASK ME

The questions every SA practice has before signing up.

Honest answers — including the things the bots don't do. Tap any question.

Is this POPIA-compliant for handling patient data? +

Yes — by design, not by accident. Three core principles: (1) The bots only touch administrative data (name, contact details, appointment time, treatment category). Never clinical notes, diagnoses, lab results or scripts. (2) All patient data lives in your own Google Sheets, in your own Google Workspace account — Jarvis Nexus never stores patient data on our infrastructure. (3) Every patient-facing message includes an explicit opt-out link, and opt-outs are honoured in real time. Full details in the privacy policy — happy to walk through it on the demo call.

Will it work with my practice management software (GoodX, Healthbridge, Vericlinic, Elixir)? +

Honest answer: it depends. Cal.com integration is native (we'd recommend Cal.com for new practices). For GoodX, Healthbridge and Vericlinic we use a Google Sheets bridge — your practice exports appointment data to a sheet, the bots watch the sheet. It's not real-time integration, it's daily sync. For Elixir or unusual systems, bring your software to the demo and I'll be honest about what's possible. Worst case: if you don't already use a digital booking system, Cal.com can be set up during onboarding at no extra cost.

Does it handle medical aid pre-authorisation? +

No — and I want to be upfront about that. Medical aid pre-auth is its own dedicated, regulated workflow (Discovery, Momentum, Bonitas etc. all have different APIs and rules). It's on the roadmap as a separate bot we'd build for Operator or Command tier clients, not part of the Healthcare Pack Starter. If pre-auth is your biggest pain right now, let's discuss a custom build on the demo call.

Will the recall messages sound pushy or salesy? +

No. The tone is your tone — warm, professional, factual. We calibrate it together during onboarding. Recall messages reference the patient's last visit by name and date ("It's been 6 months since your last cleaning with Dr. Naidoo") — not generic "we miss you" copy. One recall per cycle, opt-out always visible. If a patient marks STOP, they never get another recall message — full stop. Your reputation is the whole product; the bots are designed to protect it, not erode it.

Who has access to my patient data? +

Only you. Patient data lives in your own Google Sheets, in your own Google Workspace. Jarvis Nexus reads from it via your authorisation (you grant n8n access during onboarding, and you can revoke it any time from your Google account). I personally don't see any patient records unless you specifically share them with me to debug a problem. Practice staff who have Sheets access already (front-desk, hygienist, billing) keep that access — the bots don't change anything there.

Can it differentiate between long-time patients and new ones? +

Yes. You can mark patient categories in your sheet (new, returning, VIP, chronic care) and the bots adjust tone and cadence per category. For VIPs, the bot defers to a personal call from you instead of an automated message ("Mrs Khumalo missed her 11:00 — do you want to call her yourself before I send anything?"). For high-frequency chronic care patients, recall cadence runs at 3 months instead of 6.

What happens if a patient opts out or replies "STOP"? +

They're immediately removed from all future bot messaging — recall, rescue, everything. The bot detects "STOP", "unsubscribe", "remove me", "stop messaging me" and marks the patient's record accordingly. You get a Telegram ping so you know. You can still contact them manually for clinical reasons (that's your judgement call, not the bot's), but no automated messages go out.

I'm a single-practitioner clinic — is this overkill? +

The opposite. Solo practitioners have the highest no-show pain — you can't afford to lose even one Saturday slot, and you don't have a receptionist running recall calls every Friday. The Healthcare Pack is sized for 1-practitioner clinics doing 8–15 appointments per day. If you do 30+ appointments per day across multiple practitioners, the Operator tier (5,000 events/month, custom voice per practitioner) is the better fit. Bring your numbers to the demo and I'll size you correctly.

What about WhatsApp? Most of my patients live there. +

Honest answer: the Healthcare Pack currently sends via email only, not WhatsApp. Our WhatsApp Business integration (B1 Lead Catcher) launches Phase 3 — we're waiting on the SA WhatsApp Business account approval from Meta, which has stricter rules for healthcare senders. Until then, email reaches adult patients who check inbox daily — and for elderly patients who don't, we're upfront that a quick phone call from your front desk still works better than any digital channel until WhatsApp is live and properly cleared for healthcare. When WhatsApp goes live and we've cleared the healthcare-sender requirements, your account gets upgraded at no extra cost.
